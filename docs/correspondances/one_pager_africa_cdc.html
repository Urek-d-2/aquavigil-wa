AquaVigil-WA 🌊

Open-Source Wastewater Surveillance Pipeline for West Africa
Lomé, Togo · 2026
Target: CPHIA Nov 2026
Detecting cholera, typhoid & polio in wastewater — up to 2 weeks before hospitals are overwhelmed

The Problem

In non-sewered West African settings, hospital surveillance detects outbreaks 2–4 weeks after community spread begins. By then, response is reactive and costly.

Our Solution

  • Metagenomic sequencing of wastewater from canals, drains & markets — no sewer network required
  • Multi-pathogen detection in a single sample: V. cholerae, S. typhi, Poliovirus, AMR
  • PMMoV/CrAssphage normalization for cross-site comparability
  • Open-source Snakemake pipeline + real-time alert dashboard
  • PHA4GE-compliant data for continental interoperability

Why It Matters for Africa CDC

  • First fully open-source, sovereign WBE pipeline for non-sewered West Africa
  • Aligned with Africa CDC Continental WES Strategy (GLOWACON)
  • Designed for replication — any country with a sequencer can deploy it
