Dear Dr. [Name],

I am writing to introduce **AquaVigil-WA**, an open-source wastewater-based epidemiological (WBE) surveillance pipeline developed in Togo, targeting pathogen early-warning for West Africa, with results planned for presentation at **CPHIA Addis-Ababa, November 2026**.

**The problem we address:**
In low-resource settings like Togo, hospital-based surveillance detects epidemic outbreaks 2–4 weeks *after* community spread has begun. WBE can detect pathogen signals in wastewater **up to 2 weeks earlier**, at a fraction of the cost of clinical surveillance.

**What AquaVigil-WA does:**
- Metagenomics sequencing of wastewater samples (canals, open drains, market areas — environments without formal sewer networks)
- Automated multi-pathogen detection: *V. cholerae*, *S. typhi*, Poliovirus, AMR indicators — in a single sample
- Signal normalization using PMMoV/CrAssphage biomarkers for quantitative comparability across sites
- Open-source Snakemake pipeline (GitHub: https://github.com/Urek-d-2/aquavigil-wa)
- PHA4GE-compliant data output for interoperability with continental surveillance systems
- Real-time alert dashboard (FastAPI + Leaflet.js) for public health officers

**Why this matters for Africa CDC:**
- First fully open-source, sovereign WBE pipeline designed specifically for non-sewered West African environments
- Directly aligned with the Africa CDC Continental WES Strategy (GLOWACON framework)
- Designed for replication: any country with a sequencer and internet connection can deploy it
- Pilot: 5 sampling sites across Lomé, Togo — results by October 2026
